# Scrap-Sales-Report-
I created a scrap sales performance dashborad using Power BI. 

Summary

The Scrap Sales Performance Dashboard presents a comprehensive overview of scrap material sales activity recorded between January 2022 and July 2025. The dashboard consolidates data from 1,098 transactions across seven material categories and visualizes key performance indicators including total revenue, quantity sold, VAT collected, and average unit pricing.

##Project Insights

conclusion

1.Revenue is heavily concentrated in one category
Cardboard alone contributes 64.92% of all revenue. This is a significant concentration risk — if cardboard prices fall or supply reduces, the entire operation's revenue would be severely impacted. The business should consider diversifying into higher-value categories such as Metal and Electronic waste which currently contribute less than 2% of revenue despite potentially higher unit prices.

2 External buyers drive the entire business
The bar chart shows CICL Employees and CCS generating essentially zero revenue compared to External buyers. This means the business is entirely dependent on its external buyer relationships. Losing key external clients would have an immediate and severe impact on revenue. Building and maintaining strong external buyer relationships should be a top priority.

3 Strong year-on-year growth from 2022 to 2024
Revenue grew from Rs 2.8M in 2022 to Rs 6.0M in 2023 and Rs 8.3M in 2024, representing a 196% increase over two years. This indicates successful business expansion. The 2025 figure of Rs 5.3M covers only seven months (January to July), which when annualised projects to approximately Rs 9.1M — suggesting continued growth.

4 Recycling dominates over reuse
82.87% of revenue comes from recycled materials versus 17.12% from reused materials. From a sustainability and environmental compliance perspective, this is a positive indicator. However, reused materials typically command higher prices than recycled ones. Increasing the proportion of materials directed to reuse rather than recycling could improve revenue per unit of material processed.

5 High month-to-month revenue volatility
The line chart shows significant spikes and drops in monthly revenue throughout the entire period. This volatility makes cash flow planning difficult. The business would benefit from securing longer-term supply contracts with buyers to smooth revenue across months and reduce dependence on spot sales.

6 Hazardous materials are a small but important segment
Only 21 out of 1,098 transactions (approximately 1.9%) involve hazardous materials. While this is a small proportion, hazardous materials require special handling, legal compliance, and approved buyer documentation. The Hazard Status slicer on the dashboard allows management to monitor this segment separately and ensure all regulatory requirements are being met.

Recommendations
   
•	Increase investment in collecting and processing higher-value scrap categories such as Metal, Electronic, and E-Waste to reduce revenue dependence on Cardboard.

•	Establish long-term contracts with key external buyers to reduce month-to-month revenue volatility visible in the line chart.

•	Investigate opportunities to redirect more materials from recycling to reuse, as reuse typically yields higher revenue per unit.

•	Monitor hazardous material transactions closely using the Hazard Status slicer to ensure ongoing regulatory compliance.

•	Set a formal annual revenue target — the gauge chart currently shows a Rs 45M maximum scale. A realistic near-term target of Rs 12M for 2025 (full year) would be appropriate given the current growth trend.

•	Explore developing the CICL Employee and CCS buyer channels which currently contribute negligible revenue, as internal channels can provide more predictable and stable sales.
